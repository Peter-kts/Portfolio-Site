About My Site

Purpose & Strategy:

I needed a website to showcase my work, and to define who I am as a programmer / person. The plan was to make it different and have a certain style to it, but not Avant Garde to the point that it ruins user experience.

I tried to avoid over-doing effects and interactive "games" because I figured any visitors would just find them annoying, and wouldn't actually add to the website in a meaningful way. I had to come up with something that was functional, but different.

Process:

First, I started off by trying to think outside the box; I went through about three designs for the homepage. The idea I had was a controllable character (using the arrow keys) that could interact with the website in some way.

It was a simple easter egg where the character could punch a heavy bag, then it was a Mario-esque running mini-game where the user navigated through the menu by jumping and hitting the corresponding menu item. However, this wasn't really a great idea. For one, It was a lot of work for very little benefit, for a feature most people would not even notice or use. Second, it wouldn't even work on mobile; unless I created virtual keys for the mobile user to press, while doing this mini-game would be great as a side project; I don't think it had any place on the homepage of my personal website.

After going through those designs, I decided on something simple, emulating the ever-popular google flat style, but also putting a twist on it. I tried to think of shapes, colors, styles that weren't in many websites. All of a sudden it clicked and I quickly drew up a loose framework for this site, using octogons, hexagons, and vertical text. What you see before you is the end result.

The site uses a Django backend that runs off of Python. Due to the fact that I'm the only person who will be editing the site, it doesn't need an intense content management system. Django serves all the purposes I need, and will make it easy if I ever want to expand and create a blog.

Conclusion:

I am content with the website I've created, while it does not have extremely flashy effects or showcase the apex of my programming skills, I feel it doesn't need to. Over designing and forcing in needless effects can often lead to the opposite of the desired outcome. Instead of creating a beautiful design with seamless UX and UI, it might just weigh the site down, make it slow, clunky and confusing to use. The last thing I want when someone visits a site I've made is for them to not know how to navigate it.
